Butterfly: A Social App Design

Home / Projects /Butterfly

Project Details

During a brainstorming sprint, our team silently voted on an impactful social app meant for Austinites to socialize with a purpose and turn networking into potential friendships.

  • My Role: Project Manager, Presentation Coordinator, UX Designer, UX Researcher, Content Writer, Content Editor
  • Tools: Adobe Creative Cloud, InVision Studio, Sketch, Miro, Trello, Google Drive, Excel, PowerPoint

View Case Study

First Steps:

We compiled data analysis and interviews before forming a framework for early designs.

Proto Personas

Everything begins with a story. We had to place ourselves into the shoes of a potential user and understand their pains, goals, and story.

This sets the stage as we identify the problem.

User Interviews and Surveys

We formulated an interview plan and found users in coffee shops, around classrooms, and on the streets. We also sent out a survey that reached over forty users.

This step helped us to understand the needs and motivations of users.

Defining the User and Problem

We took our research and silently grouped data before analyzing as a group. This gave us a more in depth understanding of users.

This enabled us to update the proto persona into a user persona. From there, we defined the problem and potential solutions.

Next Steps:

We prioritized needs, continued research with competitors, and visualized the user journey.

Feature Prioritization

We honed in on top aspects brought out by user needs. This began with silent dot voting that turned into a priority matrix to pinpoint the top 3-4 focal points.

Competitor Analysis and BMC

Our next step was taking a detailed look at the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats to both direct and indirect competitors.

Then we developed an overview in a business model canvas.

User Journey

Once we had a layout of the user storyboard, we formulated a user scenario.

Onward to Early Designs...

We developed a user task flow with 3 main goals to guide our design and set a foundation for the testing to come.

Each team member created their own paper wireframes. Through A/B and user testing, we narrowed down on best features.

Prototypes and Testing...

We used UI skills, such as color psychology, button placement, accessibility, and information hierarchy, and compiled a style guide for consistent design as we turned our wireframes into a clickable low-fidelity prototype.

With a user testing plan in place, we set out to guerilla test users.

After compiling test results, we made iterations and a final high-fidelity prototype.
